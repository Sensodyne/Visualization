What is Project MaPPPing?

Project MaPPPing is one of SDP’s major initiatives in line with the UN SDG goals to contribute to the development of sustainable and resilient infrastructures. We believe that Public Private Partnership (PPP) is the most efficient way of building infrastructures, so we aim to provide relevant data on PPP infrastructure projects.

The question of why SDP is working on Project MaPPPing also matters. First, why, of all things, do we focus on infrastructure? The correlation between infrastructure and sustainable development is key for our project. Not to mention “goal 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure” and “goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities” are directly related to infrastructure out of the SDGs, other goals such as clean water all set forth as a premise a solid infrastructure.

About us

Uniqueness

Our map is distinct from other maps because it is a ‘Failure Map’ that specifically focuses on the delayed and or canceled PPP projects by tracking them down, gathering relevant information, and visualizing the data in a way so that anyone can easily understand the dynamics of the field. We collect information about ‘failed’ PPP deals that have occurred in all countries regardless of their income or size and of every type of infrastructure projects including power plants, wind farms, roads, water utilities and more.

User Experience

Our map aims to maximize the experience of our map users not only by providing specific reasons to why a project was delayed/ canceled, but also by providing various filtering services so that users can search for specific projects based on its location, type, status, year and more. Furthermore, our AI tool suggests similar PPP projects that users might take interest in based on their search results. Overall, we hope that users can gather various insights using our map and support the development of sustainable infrastructures for our sustainable future.

Our Focus

Out of many projects aimed at building infrastructure projects, SDP focuses on PPP. This is due to the efficiency of PPP projects. Building an infrastructure requires money, most of the time an excessively heavy burden for governments in developing nations. Partnership with the private sector helps share the expenses to reach a more efficient way of investing in projects. Government’s overall involvement in the project makes it easier for the PPP project to be cleaner, society-friendly, compared to when a profit seeking corporation takes the lead alone.

Why a Failure map?

Failure is the pillar of success; this applies to sustainable development as well. Knowing the causes of failures can become the milestone for not repeating that mistake in the future. Project MaPPPing contributes effectively to this since the map is a highly efficient way of studying the tendency of failure causes that can be grouped into regions, types of PPP, and more. The reason SDP is working on a map is the the power of image that a map has as its strength. Information acquired through maps are more intuitive. Furthermore, the user is able to accrue insights oneself using the various filters that our map provides. Through such offers of information SDP aims to contribute to the promotion of PPP. Project owners, investors, participants, and other stakeholders will find our PPP visualization failure map unique in its service to their future projects.


Insight

SDP Failure Map provides insights as follows. This section enables the potential users to understand and foresee how the map can be utilized.

The causes of failure of the PPP infrastructure projects

The categorized causes of project failures show potential risks and allow project managers to devise numerous failure scenarios and prepare effective recovery strategies.

Areas with high project failure rates and low areas

  • Investors may refer to the data to make their infrastructure project investment decisions.

  • The data could work as the starting point of the feasibility check for the consulting firms.

Number of project failures per year

Scholars and researchers of various organizations can detect the annual growth of the infrastructure projects in the world.

Frequent project failure cause by sector

Corporates and governments may prevent possible failure cause with sector-specific plannings.


Varying degree of failure in developing/developed countries (Does the degree of national development have a significant impact on failure)?

Banks, corporations, and government agencies can use this data as a means of determining risks in deciding whether to invest in certain countries or not.

Impact of major crisis (e.g. COVID-19) on project failure gradient

Analysts and investment consultants can use this data to predict how current/future crises will affect projects.

Project failure over time (can be linked to IMF, Covid, etc. when data is more accumulated)

When more data is accumulated, project failure over time may be linked with general global circumstances.

Concentrated sector by country/region

National governments, investors and other stakeholders may use this data to determine which infrastructure sector is vulnerable in terms of country or region.

Methodology

SDP Project MaPPPing Failure map focuses on Private Public Partnership project deals that have been affected by various regions reported by open sources shared on the web. The team categorized and organized the data based on significant characteristics of the project and laid them out on the map for users to view. These characteristics generally follow World Bank PPI classification as well as what the team believes is significant. The Failure Map utilizes the filter bar based on these characteristics and classifications.
